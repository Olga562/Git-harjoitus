Culture of Bali
Enchanting Bali

Bali's rich and vibrant culture is deeply rooted in its Hindu heritage, which sets it apart from the rest of Indonesia. Balinese people strongly adhere to their religious beliefs and rituals, and you can witness their devotion in the numerous temples, colorful ceremonies, and daily offerings that adorn the island. Even the simplest inns are beautifully decorated with Balinese art, and the morning always begins with a prayer and a sacrificial gift on an ornate home altar.
Temples are everywhere, and Bali's finest temples are truly mystical-looking apparitions. Almost every day of the year, a traditional temple festival takes place somewhere on the island, where tourists are also welcome.

Food

Bali's culinary scene is a delightful fusion of flavors, showcasing a diverse range of dishes that cater to all tastes. Balinese cuisine draws inspiration from Indonesian, Indian, Chinese, and European influences, resulting in a unique blend of spices, herbs, and techniques. Rice is the cornerstone of Indonesian cuisine, including Balinese cuisine, and it is a staple ingredient that forms the foundation of most meals. Rice dishes are usually served with fish, chicken, pork or beef and a portion of vegetables.
Nasi Goreng is iconic Indonesian dish, often considered the national dish of Indonesia, is a flavorful fried rice preparation. Balinese Nasi Goreng typically includes shrimp, chicken, or vegetables, combined with aromatic spices, kecap manis (sweet soy sauce), and topped with a fried egg. It is commonly served with shrimp crackers and pickled vegetables.
